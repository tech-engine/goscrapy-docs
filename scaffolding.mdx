---
title: "Project Scaffolding"
description: "Understand the generated files and their purpose"
---

When you run `goscrapy startproject <project_name>`, GoScrapy generates a structured boilerplate designed for maintainability and scale.

Here are the files created and their purpose:

- **`main.go`**: The entry point. Initializes the spider and runs it.
- **`settings.go`**: Centralized configuration. Defines your pipelines (e.g., CSV, JSON) and middlewares (e.g., Retry, Dupefilter).
- **`base.go`**: Engine setup. It hides the boilerplate of wiring the Engine, Scheduler, and Worker Pool together.
- **`spider.go`**: Contains your core parsing logic (`Open`, `Close`, `parse`). This is where you spend most of your time.
- **`record.go`**: Defines your data model (structs). **Crucially, the struct tags defined here are necessary not only for automatic extraction but also for exporting data when using pipelines.**
- **`job.go`** & **`errors.go`**: Utility files for custom job configurations and error handling.
- **`constants.go`**: Central place for URLs, selectors, or API keys.
